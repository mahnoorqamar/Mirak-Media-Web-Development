How to Colorize Black and White Image in Photoshop

  1. Home
  2. blog

Written by : Mahnoor Qamar

10/01/2023

Introduction

Today, we embark on a comprehensive journey, unraveling the intricacies of transforming a monochrome image into a dazzling kaleidoscope of colors. This step-by-step guide will equip you with the skills to breathe life into your black and white masterpieces. So, let's delve into the enchanting realm of colorization and watch the magic unfold!

1. Setting the Stage

Our journey begins with a careful selection process. Choose a captivating black and white image from Unsplash, the link conveniently provided in the description below. This image will be the blank canvas onto which we'll paint our technicolor dreams. Additionally, pick a colorized image with similar lighting conditions; this will be our guiding star throughout this creative expedition.

2. Safeguard Your Work

Before diving into the artistic process, it's prudent to protect your progress. Duplicate the main image layer and stash away the original for future reference. Overlay the chosen colorized image onto the canvas, christening it 'Reference Image.' This initial step lays the foundation for the creation of our color palette.

3. Crafting the Color Palette

Prepare to witness the magic unfold! Armed with the eyedropper tool, set the sample size to 5x5, and sample from all layers. Zero in on the key colors - highlights, midtones, and shadows - from the reference image. Channel your inner artist and delicately paint each hue onto the duplicated layer. With satisfaction, duplicate this layer onto another, dubbing it the 'Color Palette.'

4. Creating a Gradient Map for the Skin

Now, let's breathe life into the skin tones. Apply the highlight and shadow colors to their corresponding areas on the duplicated image. Reverse the gradient map, change the blending mode to 'Color,' and embark on the refinement phase by adjusting blending options for an even more lifelike appearance.

5. Colorizing Specific Elements

a. Eyes

Unleash the magic on the eyes using a hue and saturation layer. Activate the colorized option, select your desired eye color, invert the layer, and meticulously paint over the eyes.

b. T-Shirt

Repeat the process for the t-shirt, tweaking the hue and saturation until the desired color saturation is achieved. Switch the blending mode to 'Color' to seamlessly integrate it into the overall image.

c. Hair

For a vibrant mane, adjust the hue and saturation, invert the mask, and gradually paint over the hair using a brush tool with a low flow setting, ensuring a natural, nuanced appearance.

d. Lips

Introduce a hue and saturation layer, colorize the lips, and delicately paint over them. Adjust the blending mode to 'Color' for perfect harmony.

6. Refine and Perfect

In this critical phase, revisit each layer with a discerning eye. Fine-tune details, make subtle adjustments, and ensure every element seamlessly fits into the vivid tapestry you're creating. Patience is your ally in this final refining act.

Conclusion

Congratulations, you've just completed a transformative journey, turning a black and white image into a vibrant masterpiece! This comprehensive guide aims not only to enlighten but also to inspire. Share your dazzling creations on Instagram by tagging us, and don't forget to subscribe for more tutorials. Thank you for joining us on this mesmerizing expedition through the captivating wonders of Photoshop!
